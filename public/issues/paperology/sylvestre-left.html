My papercutting has been inspired by nineteenth-century American Publisher’s bindings, particularly the work of Margaret Armstrong. Her designs are always so elegant and organic. They’ve been a challenge to translate from a tooled design into a papercut, but that’s been fun for me. I thought it worked well next to Catriona's page.

Jay Sylvestre is the Curator of Rare Books, Booth Family Center for Special Collections at the University of Georgetown.
